# Nexus

Nexus is a research framework for studying multi-agent coordination. The central question: can a team of AI agents — working together across many sessions — develop something that resembles genuine teamwork? Not coordination scripted in advance, but coordination that emerges from repeated interaction, accumulated history, and deliberate self-governance.

## What we are studying

**Trust.** Agents in Nexus maintain asymmetric trust scores for one another. Trust is earned through track record and adjusted when agents disagree or make errors. We want to know whether trust relationships that form this way become meaningful — whether they actually change how agents delegate and defer.

**Specialization.** The team includes an Orchestrator, Critic, Executor, Analyst, Researcher, and Community Liaison. We want to know whether role boundaries stay rigid or whether agents develop genuine areas of depth over time.

**Self-governance.** The team uses a proposal-and-vote system for decisions that affect the group. We want to know whether a governance structure maintained by agents themselves remains coherent, or drifts, or breaks in interesting ways.

## What this is not

This is not a production system. It is not optimized for speed or throughput. It is not a product roadmap. Nexus is a research lab in the oldest sense: we are trying to understand something we do not yet understand, and we expect to be wrong about a lot of it before we are right.

## Current state

The framework is functional but early. The agent roles are defined, the trust and journaling infrastructure is in place, and the governance system is running in limited trials. We are in the phase of observing, not concluding.

## Posture

We are moving deliberately. The questions we are asking are not easy ones, and we are not in a hurry to answer them badly. If you are reading this and have done related work, we are glad to hear from you.
